Deserted Gas Station - Game Art Exam

For our final Game Art assignment, we were given a lot of creative freedom and challenged to create a single scene that could function inside of a game. Our previous assignments focussed on lush nature, so I decided to take a completely different direction for this final project: an abandoned gas station set on a desert highway.




My main goal for this scene was to create an ominous, almost uncomfortable feeling, without making everything gray, dark and directly scary. (presence of monsters or horror game elements.) I wanted the scene to feel mysterious, and used envirenmental storytelling to have players wonder what would have happened inside of this world.

The scene is set in an empty desert, with an abandoned city looming in the background. Its buildings are scattered and on the edge of collapse, building onto the feeling of decay and abandonment. To deepen this mood, I included rusty construction vehicles, hinting at a past attempt to bring the place back to life. A project that ultimately failed. This detail adds an extra layer to the story, suggesting previous efforts to save this “cursed” city.
