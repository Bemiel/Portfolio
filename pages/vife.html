Gezapt! - "Piraten Onthuld"

This was our first client project for school, where we worked with an organization called "Ik High Five!" This company aims to teach young children in Flanders and the Netherlands about traffic safety in a fun and engaging way.

Our goal was to create a web game for their platform to teach kids about basic traffic rules, like stop signs and traffic priority. We decided on a simulated city environment with "imposters" who break traffic rules. The objective of the game is for children to spot these imposters and "zap" them.

This project was made with a group of 5 all-arounders, so we all had to choose our role in the project. As I had recently done some code heavy projects, I chose to take the role of lead artist, creating all models, textures and guiding the overall artistic direction of the game.

The main challenge was combining variation with optimisation, creating city asset variations with the least amount of models and materials as possible. I chose to keep all models really low poly as the player would mostly see them from afar. To increase variation, each spawned car would choose a random car model, and car color material that also behaves differently on each model. I used shaders to give each tree a random scale, and to animate the leaves.
