Music Production

About a year ago, I started experimenting with music production in Fl Studio, which has since evolved into a huge passion of mine. This newfound skill is now making its way into my game projects as well. I recently took on the role of music creator for a group project, allowing me to apply this skill in a real, client-based setting.

As a hobby, I experiment with a wide range of genres and styles, always looking to improve and learn new techniques each day. Also teaming up with vocalists to get a feel for that side of production and create more polished, finished pieces.



Vibe For Life

For a group project for Win For Life, I took the role of audio & music engineer. For the background music, I created a system that would smoothly transition a lot of music loops into each other. Every zone then has it's unique background track that transitions when the player enters it. I isolated all percussion for the tracks as blending that wouldn't sound nice. I instead only blended the instruments and synths, and instantly transition to the next drum loop.

I tried to give every track a bit of a unique feeling and style, that matched the feeling of that area.


Camping Grounds

The camping grounds is a more relaxed area. The track should feel chill/pop-inspired as if someone in the camping grounds were playing it out of a music box.

Food Stalls

A more cluttered messy environment. I went for a more jazzy feel matching the food theme.

Party Grounds

The default area of the game. Classic party music, but not over the top.

Stage

Loud synths and reverb to create the feeling as if you were standing in front of it!

Toilets

The toilet area contains a lot of moving obstacles that require fast reflexes to dodge. To match the more tense feeling, I went for a country-inspired, almost "western-chase-scene" track.

Main Menu

The main menu has a parrot (out games' main protagonist) vibing in a hammock before they go to the party. I went for an uplifting and still party-ish track to be transitioned into even more active tracks when the game actually begins.

Game Over

A basic piano tune. I kept the percussions upbeat to keep the flow of the music throughout all transitions.

Rainbow Powerup

If the player collects the rainbow powerup, they turn into an invulnerable rainbow version of themself. To make the track feel floaty and calm, I added a soft flute instrument on top of a bunch of synth arps, for the more powerful and mysterious feel.
