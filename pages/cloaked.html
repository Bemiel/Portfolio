Cloaked

Sneak your way trough Cloaked. A stealth-based roguelike where you navigate procedurally generated dungeons, collecting loot and powerful items to help you survive. But tread carefully monsters are always on the hunt, listening and looking out for every move you make.

This game combines two pretty different genres. I experimented with procedural level generation, more complex AI using state machines, and making sound play a bigger role in the game design.

The game revolves around sneaking and dodging monsters, so the monster AI had to be a bit more complex. The enemies have multiple ways of perceiving the player, with values that show how sure they are the player is present. For example, they might see something moving in a shadowy corner but only know that something was there, not exactly what it was. They also listen for the playerâ€™s actions through SoundEvents.

Furthermore, sound! I wanted to experiment more with sound as a part of game design. Along with player feedback (like footsteps when you walk, etc.), sound becomes a key way of gathering information about your surroundings. This creates some interesting tradeoffs: sneaking allows the player to listen more carefully, whilst running plays loud footstep sounds which make it harder to hear and stay hidden.
